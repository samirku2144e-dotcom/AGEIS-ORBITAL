Mission Parameters

Welcome, Commander. The AEGIS program is designed to monitor, track, and secure our orbital infrastructure against the growing threat of Kessler Syndrome. Space is congested, and high-velocity debris poses an existential threat to satellite constellations. Your objective is to monitor the live feed, identify collision threats, and execute emergency avoidance maneuvers.

Orbital Trajectories & Post-Collision Physics

Satellites travel at roughly 7.8 km/s in Low Earth Orbit. A collision at these speeds does not merely destroy a spacecraft—it generates a massive cloud of lethal shrapnel. As shown in the schematic below, upon the point of collision, the original trajectory of the debris and the spacecraft diverge heavily, casting high-speed fragments outward. These fragments remain in orbit, creating cascading threat vectors for all other active assets.

Threat Assessment & Maneuver Pipeline

The AEGIS backend utilizes a robust Real-time Maneuver Detection algorithm. The system ingests raw TLE (Two-Line Element) orbital data to predict paths and generate tasking protocols. If a threat assessment flags an impending strike, the system shifts tracking assets pre-maneuver to observe the satellite. Once you execute a burn (Prograde, Retrograde, or Plane Shift), the mass estimation and tracking pipelines verify the successful adjustment post-maneuver.
